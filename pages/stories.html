Marketing Agency

Growing without losing control

How a 12-person digital agency eliminated 18 hours of manual work per week — without hiring a single new person.

The problem
The agency was growing fast — new clients every month, bigger retainers, more moving parts. But the ops hadn't scaled with it. Every Monday, two account managers spent half the day manually compiling reports from Google Ads, Meta, and analytics dashboards. Client onboarding was a back-and-forth email chain that took 10–14 days. Follow-ups with cold leads? Those just didn't happen.

The founder knew the ceiling was coming. "We were turning away good clients because we didn't have the bandwidth to onboard them properly."
What Aike built
Aike mapped every manual touchpoint — reports, onboarding, lead nurturing — and automated each one independently before connecting them.

Weekly reports now generate automatically every Monday at 7am: Aike pulls data from all platforms, formats it to the agency's branded template, and emails it to clients before anyone in the office has opened their laptop.

Onboarding became a 2-step process. New clients fill a single intake form; Aike creates the project workspace, sends the brief questionnaire, schedules the kickoff call, and briefs the assigned team — all without human involvement.

Lead nurturing runs on a 14-day sequence that personalizes based on the prospect's industry and engagement. Interested? Booked automatically. No response after 14 days? Moved to a low-frequency drip.

"We finally scaled without hiring another account manager. The work just gets done — I don't even think about it anymore."

— Founder, 12-person digital agency

Manufacturing

From manual ops to a real system

How a regional manufacturer cut errors by 70% and processed orders 4× faster — by replacing spreadsheets and WhatsApp with one connected ops layer.

The problem
The company had good products, loyal clients, and a serious ops problem. Order tracking lived in three different spreadsheets, supplier coordination happened over WhatsApp, and QA checks were logged in a shared Google Doc that nobody could find when something went wrong.

Errors were frequent — wrong quantities shipped, missed QA flags, supplier delays that nobody caught until the client called. Every fix required the ops manager to manually cross-reference multiple systems. "We were firefighting every single day. There was no visibility, just chaos."
What Aike built
Aike started by mapping the full order lifecycle — from incoming purchase order to shipment confirmation — and identifying every place where information moved manually.

Order management became a single source of truth: Aike pulls every incoming order, creates a structured record, and automatically assigns it to the right production line based on capacity and priority rules.

Supplier coordination moved from WhatsApp to automated notifications: Aike sends purchase orders, tracks confirmations, and escalates silently when a supplier is overdue — no manual chasing.

QA logging is now triggered at every production stage. If a check fails, Aike pauses the order, notifies the right person, and creates an audit trail automatically. Nothing gets shipped with an open flag.

"It's like we hired a full ops team without the overhead. We can see everything in real time — and the firefighting just stopped."

— Operations Manager, regional manufacturer

Founder

Removing the founder bottleneck

How one founder freed 25+ hours per week and unblocked their team — without delegating judgment they weren't ready to give up.

The problem
The company had grown to 11 people, but the founder was still the single point of failure. Every proposal needed sign-off. Every client reply went through them. Every operational decision waited in their inbox.

The team was capable, but the systems weren't. There were no clear routing rules, no response templates, no defined thresholds for what required founder input versus what the team could handle independently. The backlog kept growing. "I'd start every morning 40 messages behind and finish every night the same way."
What Aike built
Aike ran a two-week audit of every request that came through the founder — categorizing each by type, urgency, and whether it had been resolved without founder input in the past.

Routing rules now handle 80% of incoming requests automatically: client status questions go to the account lead, vendor invoices under a threshold are approved autonomously, standard proposals use pre-approved templates.

Draft generation means the founder reviews, not writes: Aike drafts responses to complex client messages based on context from previous conversations, project status, and tone guidelines. The founder approves or edits in 30 seconds.

Decision escalation is threshold-based: anything over €5k, any new contract, any HR matter — those surface immediately. Everything else resolves without interruption.

"My team can now move without waiting for me. And when something actually needs me, it's clear — I'm not buried in noise anymore."

— Founder, 11-person professional services firm
